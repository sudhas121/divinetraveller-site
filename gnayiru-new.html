Sri Pushparatheswarar Temple

PushpaRatheswarar Temple

Sri Pushparatheswarar temple at the Gnayiru village is one of the navagraha temples around Chennai dedicated to Lord Sun (Suriyan) and the prime deity here is Lord Shiva and is situated at a distance of about 15 km beyond Red Hills in the outskirts of Chennai, Tamil Nadu. The temple was praised by Sekkizhar and this temple is one of the 'Paadal Petra Sthalams'. This is also one of the 'Pancha Baskara Sthalams' meaning one of the five abodes for Lord Sun, and is revered as the prime among them.

The legend behind the temple is that once Sun was cursed by Lord Brahma. To get rid of the curse Suriya performed a penance here by bathing at the pond here and performing pooja to Lord Shiva and Goddess Swarnambikai with red lotus flowers.

Lord Shiva along with his consort Swatrnambigai appeared before Suriya and blessed him.It is beleived that every Tamil year Lord Suriya in the form rays fall on Lord and Mother during the first 7 days of the tamil New Year (April).

There is a seperate sannidhi for Lord Sun in front of the sannidhi of Lord Pushparatheswarar.

Suriyan SannithiThe Thiruvodu tree

The Moolavar of this sthalam is Sri Pushparatheswarar.

The Ambal in this sthalam is Swarnambigai .

Temple location It is on a small village called Gnayiru near Redhills off the main road

Temple Timings It is normally open from 7 am –12.30pm and 4.30pm-8.30pm

How to reach?It is situated west of Tanjore towards Tiruvayaru. There are buses from Red Hills but not very frequent. Share autos are available from Redhills.
Where to Stay?There are no hotels here. Chennai is the place to stay
Where to Eat?There are no big restaurants here. You can reach Red Hills or Chennai for lunch / dinner.
