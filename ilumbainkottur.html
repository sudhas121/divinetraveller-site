Deivanayakeswarar Temple

Sri Deivanayakeswarar temple is an ancient Shiva temple in Elimiyankottur,on the western banks of Cooum Lake, enroute to Takkolam and is around 58.0 kms from Chennai. This is one of the padal petra sthalams and the temple is more than 1300 years old.

There are many legends behind this 1000 years plus old temple, one of them is that Shiva appeared in the form of a child and then as an old man and pointed out the location of this shrine to Sambandar and his followers. The followers could not understand the cue, and again Shiva took the form of a white cow and confronted Sambandar's palanquin and eventually lead him to this temple.Sambandar is said to have headed here from Tiruvallam.

As per another legend,Shiva here is said to have been worshipped by the celestial beauty Ramba and hence the name Arambayamkottur, which over a period of time became Ilambayankottur. Another legend also has it that Chandra worshipped Shiva here.

Outside view of the Temple

The Temple: The temple is small and does not have any main tower (Rajagopuram) and has one praharam (corridors). The Shiva in this temple is a Suyambu (self manifested) facing east.This lingam is not touched by human hands, not even by the temple archakas, and is known as “Theendathirumeni”. Clothes,flowers are being offered without touching the Shivalingam. Lord Ganesha and Muruga with his consorts Valli Deivanai are seen either side of the entrance of the Karpagriha.

Yoga Dakshinamurthy

There is a seperate sannidhi for ambal Sri Kanaka Kujambikai, on the right side before entering the moolavar sannidhi. The idol of Sri Kanaka Kujambikai is very beautiful and "Santha Swaroobi". The temple priest says that Kanchi Maha Periava,whenever he visited this temple used to spend more time looking at the ambal's calm face warmly. As you come around the main sannidhi you can see Lord Dakshinamurthy on the wall on the western side facing the south.He is called as Yoga Dakshinamurthy and is seen with his right hand placed on his chest (“Chin muthirai”).There are idols of Mahavishnu, Suryan, Chandran, Brahma, Bairavar, Durgai and Chandikeswarar in the praharam (corridor)

Festivals  Apart from the regular poojas, Pradosham days are celeberated in a grand manner. Special poojas are performed during Guru Peyarchi.

Temple location The temple is around 28 kms from Kanchipuram and 58 kms from Chennai.

Temple Timings  The temple is open from 4:30 a.m. to 12:00 noon and from 5.00 p.m. to 8.00 p.m. But devotees are strongly advised to call the temple priest before visiting them. The temple priest stays in the quaters inside the temple.

How to reach?  The road is narrow and a few mini bus goes to this place and beyond. Taking your own car or a cab will be easier. You can also hire an autorickshaw from Takkolam.

Where to Stay? There are no hotels here , but there are many good hotels at all budgets in Kanchipuram.

Other notable temples nearbyOther Paadal Petra Shivasthalams near Elimiyankottur are Thakkolam (10 kms), Thiruvirkolam (16 kms), and other populat temples Thirumalpur (17 kms), Thirupachur (26 kms) and Thiruvenpakkam/Poondi (35 kms).
