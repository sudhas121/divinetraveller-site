Sri Parimala Ranganatha Perumal - Thiru Indhaloor

Sri Parimala Rangan temple

Thiruindalur is one of the 108 divya desams in Mayavaram (Mayiladuthurai) town itself.

According to the legends, Lord Narayan removed the curse of Chandran here at this place. Indhu means moon and as the Lord cured Chandra (Moon) of his curse, this place is known as Indhaloor.

Inside the temple
Sri Parimala Rangan

The Moolavar of this sthalam is Sri Parimala Ranganathan. Also called as Sugantha Vananathan and Andhiya Rangan. He is seen in Kidantha (Sleeping) Thirukkolam in Veera Sayanam facing east.

Prathyaksham for Chandran ( Moon)

The Thayaar found in this sthalam is Parimala Ranganayaki. Also known as Chandra shaba vimosana valli and Pundarigavalli.

Mangalasasanams The temple is revered by the verses of Tirumangai azhwar

Festivals This temple has utsavama (festivals) throughout the month of Aipasi (Ashwina/Ashvayuja - mid-October to mid-November).
Temple location It is situated in Mayavaram town close to the bus stand. If you drive your car, it is easily accessible.

Temple Timings It is normally open from 5 am –12.30pm and 4.30pm-8.30pm

How to reach?

Mayavaram ( Mayiladuthurai) is well connected by road, rail from Chennai, Trichy,Kumbakonam and Thanjavur


Where to Stay?There are many budget and semi deluxe hotels in Mayavaram
